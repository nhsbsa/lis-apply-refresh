You can apply online for help with health costs

What you need to know before you start

During the online application, we'll ask for information about:

  • income - how much you (or your partner) get from any jobs, pensions or benefits
  • housing costs - what you (or your partner) pay for things like Council Tax, rent and utilities
  • noticeable heartbeats (heart palpitations)
  • educational courses (if you are a student) - what and where you are studying, what your term dates are, and how your course is funded

What we will ask you to send us

If you or your partner get them, we will ask you to send us copies of your:

  • payslips
  • latest Working Tax Credit award notice
  • noticeable heartbeats (heart palpitations)
  • latest Universal Credit monthly statement

What happens after your application

Once you complete your application, you will receive a reference number for any queries you may have. Your application will then be processed and you will be contacted when a decision has been made.

If you are entitled to help, you will get either an:

  • HC2 certificate for full help with health costs – this includes free NHS prescriptions
  • HC3 certificate for partial help with health costs – you would still need to pay something towards your health costs, and need to pay for NHS prescriptions in full
