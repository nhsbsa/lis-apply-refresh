What happens next

You'll get an email from us within the next 24 hours confirming we've received your application.

We may email you asking for more information in the next few days while we assess your claim. Check your emails regularly so that your application is not delayed. Please check your junk and spam folder.

We will then let you know the outcome of your application by email.

You are not eligible for free NHS prescriptions or treatment until you receive an outcome from us confirming your eligibility. This can take up to 18 working days.

What help you could get

Depending on your circumstances, you may receive an

  • HC2 certificate (opens in new tab) for full help with health costs - this includes free NHS prescriptions
  • HC3 certificate (opens in new tab) for partial help with health costs - you would still need to pay something towards your health costs, and need to pay for NHS prescriptions in full

How to apply for a refund

If you pay NHS prescription charges, then find out later that you're entitled to free prescriptions, you may be able to claim a refund.

You'll need to ask your pharmacist for an FP57 refund receipt when you pay as you cannot get one later. Find out more about how to claim a refund (opens in new tab).

If you are claiming a refund for a sight test, you need to claim within 3 months of the date you had the test. Find out how to claim a refund (opens in new tab).
