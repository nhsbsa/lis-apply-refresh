You can apply online for help with health costs

Before you start

During the online application, we'll ask you about:

  • income - how much you (or your partner) get from any jobs, pensions or benefits
  • housing costs - what you (or your partner) pay for things like Council Tax, rent and utilities
  • educational courses (if you are a student) - what and where you are studying, what your term dates are, and how your course is funded

What we will ask you to send us

If you or your partner get them, we will ask you to send us copies of your:

  • payslips
  • latest Universal Credit monthly statement

What happens after your application

Once you complete your application, we will give you a reference number. You can use this if you need to contact us. We will process your application and contact you when we have made a decision.

If you are entitled to help, you will get either a:

  • certificate for full help with health costs (HC2) (opens in a new tab) - this includes free NHS prescriptions
  • certificate for partial help with health costs (HC3) (opens in a new tab) - you would still need to pay something towards your health costs, and need to pay for NHS prescriptions in full
